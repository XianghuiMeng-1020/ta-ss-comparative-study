[TUTOR]:   Now try this similar problem on your own without any hints. Show your reasoning step by step:

Jerry has a bunch of half-empty soda cans on his desk. He needs to drain all of them and put them in the recycling bin. He can carry four cans at once, and it takes him 39.3 seconds to drain those 5.6 cans. It takes him ten seconds each way to walk to the sink and recycling bin and then back to his desk. If there are 38.8 cans on Jerry's desk, how long does it take him to throw all of them away?
[LEARNER]: Alright, let’s solve this step by step! 😊

### Step 1: Number of trips Jerry needs
Jerry can carry **4 cans per trip**, and there are **38.8 cans** in total. To find the total number of trips required, divide the total number of cans by how many he carries per trip:

\[
38.8 \div 4 = 9.7
\]

Since Jerry can’t make part of a trip, he’ll need **10 trips** to remove all the cans.

---

### Step 2: Time per trip
Each trip involves **draining the cans** and **walking to and from the sink/recycling bin**.

- **Draining time:** From the problem, it takes **39.3 seconds to drain 5.6 cans**. So first, we calculate the draining time per can:

\[
\frac{39.3}{5.6} \approx 7.018 \text{ seconds per can.}
\]

Now, for each trip, Jerry drains **4 cans**:

\[
7.018 \times 4 \approx 28.072 \text{ seconds to drain 4 cans.}
\]

- **Walking time:** It takes Jerry **10 seconds each way**, so the round-trip walking time is:

\[
10 + 10 = 20 \text{ seconds.}
\]

- **Total time per trip:** Add the draining time and walking time:

\[
28.072 + 20 \approx 48.072 \text{ seconds per trip.}
\]

---

### Step 3: Total time for 10 trips
Now, we calculate the total time for all 10 trips by multiplying the time per trip by the number of trips:

\[
48.072 \times 10 \approx 480.72 \text{ seconds.}
\]

---

### Step 4: Convert total time to minutes
To convert **480.72 seconds** into minutes, divide by 60:

\[
480.72 \div 60 \approx 8.012 \text{ minutes.}
\]

This is approximately **8 minutes and 1 second**.

---

### Final Answer:
It will take Jerry **about 8 minutes and 1 second** to throw away all 38.8 cans! 😊
