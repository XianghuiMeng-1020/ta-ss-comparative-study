[LEARNER]: Okay, let’s break this problem down step by step and solve it logically. Here we go!

---

### Step 1: Cut the rope into four equal parts.
The total length of the rope is **250.0 meters**, and Stefan cuts it into **4 equal parts**. To find the length of each part:

\[
\text{Length of each piece after first cut} = \frac{\text{Total length}}{\text{Number of parts}}
\]
\[
\text{Length of each piece} = \frac{250.0}{4} = 62.5 \, \text{meters}.
\]

So now, there are **4 pieces**, each **62.5 meters long**.

---

### Step 2: Give half of the pieces to his mother.
Stefan gives **half of the 4 pieces** to his mother. Half of 4 is:

\[
\frac{4}{2} = 2 \, \text{pieces}.
\]

So, Stefan keeps **2 pieces**, each **62.5 meters long**.

---

### Step 3: Subdivide the remaining pieces.
Stefan now has **2 pieces**, each **62.5 meters long**. He subdivides **each of these remaining pieces** into **2 equal parts**. To find the length of the new smaller pieces:

\[
\text{Length of each smaller piece} = \frac{\text{Length of original piece}}{\text{Number of subdivisions per piece}}
\]
\[
\text{Length of each smaller piece} = \frac{62.5}{2} = 31.25 \, \text{meters}.
\]

Now, each of the **2 pieces** has been divided into **2 smaller pieces**, resulting in:

\[
2 \times 2 = 4 \, \text{smaller pieces}.
\]

Each of these smaller pieces is **31.25 meters long**.

---
