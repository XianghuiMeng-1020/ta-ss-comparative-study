[TUTOR]:   Now try this similar problem on your own without any hints. Show your reasoning step by step:

Jackson wants to improve his endurance running. His goal is to start by running 4.5 miles a day the first week, then spend the next four weeks running one additional mile/day each week. How many miles is Jackson running each day at the end of this exercise program?
[LEARNER]: Okay, I can do that! Here's the breakdown of Jackson's running program, week by week:

*   **Week 1:** Jackson runs 4.5 miles/day.
*   **Week 2:** He increases by 1 mile, so he runs 4.5 + 1 = 5.5 miles/day.
*   **Week 3:** He increases by another mile, so he runs 5.5 + 1 = 6.5 miles/day.
*   **Week 4:** He increases by another mile, so he runs 6.5 + 1 = 7.5 miles/day.
*   **Week 5:** He increases by another mile, so he runs 7.5 + 1 = 8.5 miles/day.

**Answer:** At the end of the exercise program (after 5 weeks), Jackson is running 8.5 miles each day.
