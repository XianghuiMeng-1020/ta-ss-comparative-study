[TUTOR]:   Now try this similar problem on your own without any hints. Show your reasoning step by step:

Austin is a surfer. He took a trip to the beach during surfing season and the highest wave he caught was two feet higher than four times his height. The shortest wave he caught was four feet higher than his height. The shortest wave was three feet higher than his 8.8-foot surfboard is long. How tall was the highest wave Austin caught?
[LEARNER]: Let me work through this step by step.

**Step 1: Find the height of the shortest wave**
The shortest wave was 3 feet higher than his 8.8-foot surfboard.
Shortest wave = 8.8 + 3 = 11.8 feet

**Step 2: Find Austin's height**
The shortest wave was 4 feet higher than Austin's height.
11.8 = Austin's height + 4
Austin's height = 11.8 - 4 = 7.8 feet

**Step 3: Find the height of the highest wave**
The highest wave was 2 feet higher than four times Austin's height.
Highest wave = 4 × 7.8 + 2
Highest wave = 31.2 + 2 = 33.2 feet

The highest wave Austin caught was **33.2 feet** tall.
