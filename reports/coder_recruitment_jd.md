## Study Overview

We are evaluating how different LLM prompting protocols affect the quality of
AI-generated tutoring dialogues for educational research. Coders will rate transcripts of
simulated tutoring conversations on 9 educational dimensions based on established theoretical
frameworks (Blair et al., 2007; Koedinger et al., 2015).

**All data used is de-identified. No sensitive information involved.**

## Training Schedule (Week 3–5, ~20 hrs/person)

| Session | Content | Duration |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Session 1 | Framework study: Blair (2007) + Koedinger (2015) readings + discussion | 4 hrs |
| Session 2 | Codebook walkthrough: all 9 dimensions with positive/negative examples | 4 hrs |
| Session 3 | TalkMoves warm-up: practice on 30 pre-coded utterances from TalkMoves corpus | 3 hrs |
| Session 4 | Calibration round 1: independently code 30 dialogue packets → discuss disagreements → revise codebook | 5 hrs |
| Session 5 | Calibration round 2: independently code 30 dialogue packets → ICC check (must ≥ 0.75 to proceed) | 4 hrs |

**Go/No-go gate**: Both coders must achieve ICC(2,k) ≥ 0.75 on all 9 dimensions before main coding begins.
If one dimension falls below threshold, we revise that dimension's anchors together and re-code a new set of 20 packets.

---

## Main Coding (Weeks 6–9, 60–80 hrs/person)

- **240 dialogue packets** × all 9 dimensions + use-case decision + 6 failure flags
- Estimated time per packet: 15–20 minutes
- Format: Online Qualtrics survey (one survey per packet, delivered in batches of 30)
- Blind protocol: condition labels removed; you will not see AI model names
- Weekly 1-hour check-in meeting (not for discussing individual ratings — for process/logistics only)
- No discussing specific ratings with the other coder until all 240 are complete

**Expected time**: 60–80 hours for main coding (at $25–30/hr = $1,500–2,400 total)
